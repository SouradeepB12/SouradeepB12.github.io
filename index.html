Souradeep Bhattacharya

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Engineering at Iowa State University, specializing in cybersecurity for cyber-physical systems. My research focuses on enhancing the resilience and security of critical infrastructure, particularly renewable-integrated smart grids, distributed energy resources (DERs), and electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure. My work leverages advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) real-time simulation, Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) frameworks, and cloud-based integration to develop robust solutions against emerging cyber threats.

With over 5 years of proven industry experience in building automation and project leadership, I have successfully bridged the gap between academic research and practical application. My professional background includes designing and implementing secure, efficient systems that drive innovation in the energy and automation sectors.

I have published research in the field of cybersecurity for critical infrastructures and am skilled in programming, data analysis, and advanced problem-solving. My goal is to contribute to the development of secure, sustainable, and intelligent energy systems that meet the challenges of the modern era.

Bio

Souradeep Bhattacharya (Graduate Student Member, IEEE) received the B.Tech. degree in Applied Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering from the West Bengal University of Technology (WBUT), India, in 2016, and M.S. degree in Computer Engineering from Iowa State University (ISU), USA, in 2025. He is currently working toward the Ph.D. degree in Computer Engineering in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at ISU. His research interests include cybersecurity for cyber-physical systems, smart grids, substation automation, distributed energy resources, and machine learning.

Research

My research focuses on cybersecurity for cyber-physical systems, with an emphasis on resilient and secure critical infrastructure. I am particularly interested in renewable-integrated smart grids, distributed energy resources, electric vehicle charging infrastructure, hardware-in-the-loop real-time simulation, Industrial Internet of Things frameworks, AI/ML-enabled security, and cloud-connected energy systems.
